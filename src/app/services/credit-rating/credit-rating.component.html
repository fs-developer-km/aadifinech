What Is Credit Rating?

A credit rating is an independent evaluation of your company's ability to service debt, expressed through alphanumeric categories like AAA to D. These ratings influence investor confidence, interest rates, and eligibility for term loans, bonds, or commercial paper.

Why It Matters for Your Business

Increase Loan Approvals & Improve Terms

High ratings → lower interest rates, easier access to credit.

Enhance Investor Confidence & Market Access

Use ratings to raise funds via bonds, NBFCs, or bank facilities.

Meet Regulatory Mandates

SEBI requires ratings for public debt instruments ≥ ₹100 crore.

Build Brand Reputation & Trust

Transparent credit profiles attract better partners and deal terms.

How Your Rating Impacts Debt & Drawing Power

Lower Cost of Funds

Higher ratings reduce interest rates on term loans, bonds, working capital, and letters of credit.

Increased Borrowing Capacity

Strengthened credit profile often leads to higher sanctioned limits.

No Debt on Balance Sheet

Ratings are advisory—they don't appear as debt, but improve limit utilization.

Enhanced Investor & Lender Confidence

Regular surveillance maintains transparency and credit trust.
